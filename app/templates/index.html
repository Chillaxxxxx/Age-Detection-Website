How It Works

It's pretty simple.

Upload

First things first, upload a picture of your choice with a face on it. Be sure that the image at least contains a face as that is what the AI has been trained to detect. Also be sure to only upload jpg or png files. Note: The model performs better when the image is a close-up of a face.

Wait

Wait a few seconds as the AI runs through your image. Our AI has the capability to detect ages up to 60+. It has trained with over 6000 images and has at least 50% accuracy in all age ranges.

Results

Once the AI is done processing your image, it should give you an age range and its percentage of certainty. Hopefully our AI got it right and if it did, you can keep on trying till you stump it.

Journey

The steps we took to make our Age-Detection AI

  • Step 1

    Collecting data

    We used techniques such as web scraping on google and using Kaggle datasets to put together a dataset of nearly 3000 images. After uploading the images to roboflow we then annotated the data to make sure the computer knew what to look for.

  • Step 2

    Choosing Our Model

    After having our dataset complete we had to choose which machine learning model we would use to create our Age-Detection AI. Taking into account Google Colab's restrictions and gpu load we setled for Yolov5s, a quick yet accurate model.

  • Step 3

    Training Our Model

    After we picked out Yolov5s we chose our training parameters and used Google Colab to run our mdoel training. We also used WandB in order to track our results.

  • Step 4

    Designing Our Website

    Having our model ready to go the last step was to create a presentable website for others to try out our AI! With our Frontend and Backend team working in tandem we designed this website with our model integrated.

Purpose

Why we made our AI

Our group started with several interesting project ideas, such as identifying food, identifying camouflaged animals, and identifying pill medication. After weighing in the product specs and the time given for this project, we ultimately decided that creating an ai to identify age would be a sizeable goal. And with the several possible roadblocks surrounding this idea, it also seemed like a great challenge. An AI that can identify age has its applications. Knowing the age is very essential in medical assistance. The older a person is, the more likely they are to have health conditions or harmful drug interactions. This AI would be useful in identifying the age of a patient with no identification, ensuring proper treatment. Age is also essential in the identification of criminals caught on security cameras. If DNA or fingerprints are inconclusive, this AI would at least help create a more accurate profile of a criminal to release to the public.

Statistics

Some of our AI data

This graph is known as a confusion matrix. It essentially displays the accuracy of our AI. On the X-Axis we have the actual labels used for known ages of images from our dataset. On the Y-Axis we have the predicted age range from our AI. The decimal numbers are percentages of guesses out of 1 for a specific age range. With this we can see which age ranges have lower accuracy and which of the age ranges the AI is getting it confused with. Due to our limited time and data and the fact that many different factors affect how old people look, our model isn't always accurate.

PR-curve

Evaluating model performance

A high precision value means there were very few false positives and that the classifier is more permissive in the criteria for classifying something as negative. A high recall value means there were very few false negatives and that the classifier is more permissive in the criteria for classifying something as positive. The higher the curve, the more accurate the model classified the age range. Meaning the closer the line is to the top right of the graph, the better the overall predictions will be. This was used to determine if the AI required more or different training.

Dataset

Collecting and Labeling our data

In order to obtain the hundreds of images needed for an AI as complicated as facial age recognition, we had to learn about many different ways of sourcing. The two main ways we found images for our dataset was through online databases and web scraping. Kaggle provided databases with thousands of images for frontal facing faces. With multiple different types of databases our group split off into different age ranges to clean through the dataset for clear and useful images. When it came time to add images to our dataset we had to get some of our images from web scraping to ensure that they were different. Web scraping is the process of obtain every google image on a specific page in one easy to clean through area.

Retraining

Data Augmentation and Parameters

Before our first round of training, we did research on type of data augmentation and parameters we should be using. Parameters such as the size of images used for training(dimensions), the amount of times the AI should go through the training set(known as epochs), the amount of images each grid should include(batch size) and the type of model we should use(YOLOv5). To determine the best types of data augmentation for our dataset we first did some research on the types of augmentation and what each of them accomplish . We found blur, scaling, rotation, contrast and brightness, projective rotation, grey scale, cropping and cutouts to be more useful .These augmentations serve to lengthen and add diversity to a dataset so that our AI isn’t biased. Each of our 6 group members choose 2 or 3 specific ones to individually train an AI for trial and error. We had some overlap and some differences so that we can see the different effects. With the data collected from the different tests we have determined what augmentations we will be using for the training of our final product and got insight on how to further improve our dataset.
